100 years of the theramin

2019 marks the 100th anniversary of the theremin, and we celebrate this by inviting Dorit Chrysler to Copenhagen for a concert and a workshop.

We ask anyone to contribute in the celebration with talks or workshops that explore the history of the theremin and/or its impact on electronic music.

Contact: jwa@create.aau.dk

Deadline: December 15th 2018

Queen tribute concert

Let’s cover some Queen! On the final day of the festival, we want anyone musically inclined to channel their inner Freddie Mercury.

We hope to see music created with physical modeling, self made instruments, real world sampling, audience participation, interesting visuals, etc.

We only ask that:

  • You play songs from the Queen repertoire
  • You incorporate the spirit of art, science, technology, and digital creativity

If you have the music, but lack the singing (or vice versa), let us know and we’ll try to help with the pairing. Or maybe you just want to contribute with cool visuals? Let us know.

Contact: jwa@create.aau.dk

Deadline: December 15th 2018

Installations at AAU campus

The Aalborg University Copenhagen building has some interesting features that could lend itself to interesting art or technology installations.

We encourage anyone to submit suggestions to installations with sound, technology, lighting, sensors, projection, etc. that utilise some of the characteristic areas of AAU CPH:

  • We are situated next to the water.
  • An arched bridge connects the two buildings.
  • The main entrance has steps flanked by a cascading fountain.
  • The reception has a spiral staircase and an open space that leads 5 stories up in the air.
Contact: jwa@create.aau.dk

Deadline: December 15th 2018

Sonic or visual navigation at AAU campus

Lead visitors to and from points of interest in the AAU building during the Queens Festival. Create the “trail of breadcrumbs” that will allow visitors to find their way to the workshops, talks, and installations at AAU.

  • The single longest trips will be approximately 75 meters, the shorter ones 10-15 meters.
  • Preferably, users shouldn’t be required to use a piece of technology (e.g. a smartphone) to receive information on how to go from A to B.
Contact: jwa@create.aau.dk

Deadline: December 15th 2018

What is EASTN?

The European Art - Science - Technology Network (EASTN) for Digital Creativity is a project that emerged from several European institutions involved in research, technology development, creation and education in the field of technologies applied to artistic creation. At European Level, this network is coordinated by ACROE (Grenoble, France) and supported by the Culture Program of the European Union since November 2017.

Learn more about EASTN

Queens Festival
Copenhagen
May 12th-16th, 2019

The Queens Festival takes place in Copenhagen from the 12th to 16th of May 2019 at Aalborg University Copenhagen. The festival celebrates everything that EASTN-DC is about: art, science, technology, and digital creativity with workshops, art installations, concerts, and theatrical performances.

Small Composers

In ‘Small Composers’ children in age group 1st-5th grade are invited to delve into the world of classical music and its composers. Students participate in playful musical expression and becomes a part of simple rhythmic and melodic figures. They use body, voice and available sound sources in the creative musical work and produce sound illustrations and sound scenery of their own stories. They listen attentively to short pieces of music. They get the opportunity to experience and evaluate artistic quality.

Theramin concert w/Dorit Chrysler

Dorit Chrysler has been dubbed a superior wizard of the theremin. An Austrian-born, New York based composer and sound artist, Chrysler is the co-founder of the New York Theremin Society and founder of the first school of theremin, KidCoolThereminSchool. As much as the theremin is a tool in Chrysler’s electronic instrument arsenal, she's also one the most visible thereminists spreading the gospel of this mysterious sounding instrument, which is basically played by massaging thin air.

Location:
Aalborg University Copenhagen campus

Kid Cool Theremin School w/Dorit Chrysler

Kid Cool Theremin School is dedicated to inspire and nurture creative learning and expression through innovative music education, art and science. As part of the music and education department of Pioneer Works Center for Arts and Innovation, NY and the NY Theremin Society, the Kid Cool Theremin School nurtures an environment devoted to creative discourse and collaboration for children and parents alike, and is working towards participation rather than being passive spectators through a deep integration of the arts. Without pre consisting playing techniques and the primal and unusual way of how a theremin is being played, it enhances autonomous and creative thinking and opens up a new universe of expression for parents and children alike.

Location:
Aalborg University Copenhagen campus

Black Queen concert

The Black Queen is a theatrical experience employing the tools of technology, classical music, folk melodies, contemporary poetry, and historical exploration to tell the story of Barbora of Celje, Queen of the Holy Roman Empire in the early 1400’s. She was a shrewd businesswoman and a compassionate guardian of justice. In partnership with a trusting and loving husband, King Sigismund of Luxembourg, she became the most powerful queen of her time. As Sigismund was dying, a rumor campaign was created to destroy her reputation and rob her of her powers. Even the pope helped in spreading stories of her as a heretic, a whore, and a witch. When her husband died, she was arrested and exiled. After her death she lived on only as an evil myth, The Black Queen. This project is designed to resurrect her as the badass she truly was.

Location:
Frederiksholm Kirke

Salt Itinerary

Salt Itinerary performance transcends theatrical and music conventions in a combination of text and music, where the voice is at the core of the staging dramaturgy, multiplying itself through audio and video. This stage work by Miguel Azguime developed the combination of text and music, extending Azguime's concept of “speech as music” and “music as speech”, growing in several directions (notably by means of technology - both audio and video) and embodying different realms (spoken, sung, hypervoice). It revolves around languages, words as meaning and words as sound.

Location:
Aalborg University Copenhagen campus
